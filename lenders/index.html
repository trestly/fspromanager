Loan officer FAQ

The questions we hear from LOs and brokers about third-party P&L preparation.

What is a P&L for a bank statement loan?
Many bank statement loan programs let a self-employed borrower qualify using 12 or 24 months of bank statements plus a profit & loss statement, instead of tax returns. The P&L organizes the borrower's deposits and business expenses into a standard income picture — ours are categorized to Schedule C standards with owner draws and transfers separated out.
Can a third-party bookkeeper prepare the P&L for my borrower?
Many bank statement programs accept a P&L prepared by a third-party bookkeeping or tax professional rather than the borrower themselves — but requirements vary by lender and program, so check your specific guidelines. We prepare P&Ls as a bookkeeping service, deliver them with preparer contact information, and support underwriter questions on the file.
Do I need a 12-month or 24-month P&L?
It depends on your program: 12-month bank statement programs need a P&L covering the same 12 months, while 24-month programs use the longer lookback — which also gives underwriting a year-over-year view when income trends are in question. We prepare both ( for 12 months, for 24).
How fast is turnaround?
from the time we receive complete statements, and every P&L is reviewed by a human before it reaches you. Rush situations — clear-to-close dates, expiring locks — flag it in the intake form and we'll tell you honestly whether we can hit your date.
Do you only work with Maryland loan officers?
We're based in Wheaton and work in person with loan officers across Montgomery County — Rockville, Bethesda, Gaithersburg, Silver Spring, and the wider DMV. The work itself is fully remote-friendly, so lenders anywhere in the U.S. can send files.
What does it cost?
