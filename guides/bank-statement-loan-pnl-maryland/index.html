Bank Statement Loan P&L Preparation in Maryland: How It Works for Loan Officers

FOR LOAN OFFICERS & BROKERS · UPDATED JULY 2026

Montgomery County runs on self-employed income — contractors, consultants, medical and legal practices, gig operators, small business owners across Rockville, Bethesda, Gaithersburg, Silver Spring, and Wheaton. That makes bank statement loans a core tool for Maryland LOs, and the P&L the document most likely to stall the file.

The local bottleneck

The borrower who needs a bank statement loan is usually the borrower whose books are behind — that's often why tax returns undersell their income in the first place. Asking them to produce a program-compliant P&L themselves, or to get a busy accountant to engage mid-year, routinely adds weeks to a purchase timeline in a market where listings don't wait.

How a prepared P&L closes the gap

A bookkeeping service can take the same 12 or 24 months of statements already in your file and return a P&L built for lending: revenue organized by deposit source, expenses mapped to Schedule C categories, owner draws and transfers separated out, monthly and trailing-period views, and the categorized detail behind every line for when underwriting asks. Because it's built from the statements, it reconciles to them — which is exactly what deposit-tolerance checks look for.

One requirement to verify first: programs differ on who may prepare the P&L. Where your program accepts a third-party-prepared P&L, a bookkeeper can handle it end to end. Where a CPA or EA signature is required, organized books still get the borrower to that signature dramatically faster.
